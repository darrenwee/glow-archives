GLOW 2015

Paris, 
15-18 
April 2015






The 38th GLOW colloquium will take place on April 15-18, 2015 in Paris. It will be hosted by the lab Structures Formelles du Langage (University of Paris 8 and CNRS) and co-organized with the Laboratoire de Linguistique Formelle (University of Paris Diderot and CNRS), the Institut Jean Nicod (Ecole Normale Superieure, EHESS and CNRS), and the INALCO.

This year's GLOW colloquium consists of a main conference (on April 15-17) and two thematic workshops (on April 18th). The main conference has no specific topic, so scholars are invited to submit and present papers within all areas of generative linguistics.  The call for papers for the main conference is available here. The two thematic workshops  are:
 — The implications of computation and learnability for phonological theory
 — States and events
Check out the workshops' pages for the corresponding call for papers. 

On this website, you will also find information on how to reach the conference venue and on accommodations in Paris.

For any question, you can contact the GLOW 2015 organizing committee at 38thglow2015@gmail.com.


Important dates:
First call for papers: June 15, 2014
Second call for papers: September 15, 2014
Third call for papers: November 15, 2014
Abstract submission deadline: December 1, 2014
Notification to authors: February 15, 2015
Main conference dates: April 15-17, 2015
Workshop date: April 18, 2015

 
Due to high security alert in France, you might be asked for an ID at the entrance of the conference venue and your bag might be inspected by security personnel
